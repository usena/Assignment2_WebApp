Catch Gangster Game

This “Catch Gangsters” project is a program I modeled after a game in episode 560 of a Korean variety show, “Running Man”. The game started off with one player getting assigned as a police officer. Then, the rest of the players, who are gangsters, need to choose one of five rooms to enter individually. Each of the rooms has a different amount of rewards. Rewards are in the form of money, bottles and gems. Finally, the police officer can choose two rooms that he/she thinks the gangsters are in, to enter. If there is one gangster in the room, the police will automatically seize the possessions (bottle and gem) of that gangster. If there are two or more gangsters, each of the gangsters will have to choose one of three cards: bribe, silent, and snitch. “Bribe” means the gangster needs to give one bottle to the police and can get out of the room unscathed. If all gangsters “silent” together, they get to divide the rewards in the room amongst themselves equally. But if there are both gangsters who choose “silent” and “snitch”, the gangsters who choose “silent” will lose all their possessions except money to the police. On the other hand, the gangsters who choose “snitch” will divide all the rewards amongst themselves. However, if all gangsters choose “bribe”, they will lose all their possessions except money to the police. These rewards (money, bottles, and gems) can be used to buy points which will be used to determine the winner.
